My Grandfather's Story

By: Maxwell Gullickson

Špišić Bukovica:

This map shows the Croation town of Špišić Bukovica where my mothers family lived before the outbreak of WW2. They had to leave this town during the war due to threats of their safety and persecution by German forces. The majority of my mothers cousins remained in the area or escaped to elsewhere in the country before eventually leaving during the Yugoslav Wars.

Braunau am Inn:

This town in Austria served as a Displaced Persons (DP) camp during and after WW2. Because of their status as refugees, my grandfather was born in the DP camp in this town and lived in this state until he was three years old.

Eureka, CA:

After my Oma and her family immigrated to the United States in 1951, my grandfather became a citizen of the United States in 1957 and began his education to become a doctor. Most of the Fritz family is split between Eureka, CA and Centralia, WA where my mother is from. Over time and age my grandfather has told a plethora of stories that he was passed down concerning the events that took place during his families immigration to the United States, so I have put together this map to show to him.
